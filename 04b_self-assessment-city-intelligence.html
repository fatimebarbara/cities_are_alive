🔍 Self-Assessment

Evaluate Your
City Intelligence Approach

A reflection-first instrument to discover your dominant postures, your underdeveloped ones, and where your growth edge lies.

This is not a test. There are no right answers. Rate each statement honestly — 1 means "this is not how I currently work" and 5 means "this describes me closely". The goal is self-knowledge, not a perfect score. The most valuable result is discovering which postures you underuse.

⚙️
Optimiser How strongly do I operate in evidence-driven, efficiency-focused mode?
When facing an urban challenge, I instinctively reach for data and evidence before forming an opinion.
Rarely Always
I find it natural to set measurable targets and track whether interventions are working.
Rarely Always
I can identify inefficiencies in processes and propose evidence-based improvements without being asked.
Rarely Always
🌉
Translator How naturally do I bridge difference, connect silos, and surface what data misses?
When I enter a stakeholder conversation, I notice who is absent and what perspective is missing.
Rarely Always
I can reframe technical findings in language that resonates with communities who don't use technical language.
Rarely Always
I actively work to connect people across institutional silos who have information that, together, would produce better decisions.
Rarely Always
🌿
Steward How consistently do I hold long-term thinking in short-term environments?
When a fast decision is being pushed through, I pause to ask what it forecloses for future generations.
Rarely Always
I make the case for protecting community dignity and belonging as measurable strategic assets, not just values.
Rarely Always
I regularly think about path dependency — how today's choices open or close tomorrow's options.
Rarely Always
👂
Systems Listener How attuned am I to weak signals, patterns, and what data doesn't capture?
I regularly notice weak signals — subtle shifts in behaviour, tone, or social patterns — before they become visible crises.
Rarely Always
When I observe a behaviour, I instinctively ask what feedback loop or systemic dynamic is producing it.
Rarely Always
I take qualitative and experiential data seriously — community mood, felt insecurity, collective emotion — as important system signals.
Rarely Always
