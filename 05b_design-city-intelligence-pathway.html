Where you are now

Honest starting points produce honest pathways.

The starting question

Before you can chart a meaningful direction, you need to know your actual position — not the one you want to be in, or think you should be in. This step asks for honesty, not aspiration.

Describe your current role and context in 2–3 sentences

Which City Intelligence posture do you rely on most — and which do you underuse?

What is the most significant gap between how you currently work and how the City Intelligence approach would have you work?

🔄

The shift you commit to

One real change is worth more than ten aspirations.

Why one shift?

The City Intelligence approach changes how you see — but sustained practice changes how you act. Committing to one concrete, specific shift is more powerful than a list of intentions. Name the shift clearly enough that you would know, three months from now, whether you made it.

What is the one mindset or practice shift you are committing to?

What will be different in how you work, 90 days from now, if you make this shift?

What will make this shift hard — and what is your plan for those moments?

⚡

Your first intelligent move

The smallest action that shifts the system — yours and your city's.

The First Intelligent Move principle

Your First Move is not the biggest thing you could do. It is the most precisely placed thing you can do this week that begins to shift a pattern — in your own practice, in your team, or in your city context. Small and precisely placed beats large and scattered.

What is your First Intelligent Move — the specific action you will take in the next 7 days?

What CII pillar does this move address?

How will you know this move has worked?

🌿

Sustaining the practice

Intelligence is not a destination — it is a continuous practice of stewardship.

Practice, not project

The City Intelligence approach is not a tool you use and put down. It is a way of seeing that needs to be exercised regularly to stay sharp. This step asks you to design the conditions that will keep your practice alive — through structure, accountability, and intention.

Who will you practice with? (A colleague, a peer cohort, a mentor)

What regular practice will you embed? (A weekly habit, a monthly ritual, a quarterly review)

What is your commitment to the cities and communities you work with?
