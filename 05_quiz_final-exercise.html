✅ Final Exercise

Define Your First
Intelligent Intervention

Four questions. One complete, concrete, systemic action — in your own city context. This is where the course becomes practice.

Exercise progressStep 1 of 4
This is not a knowledge test. There are no right answers. This exercise asks you to synthesise everything from the course — the lens shift, the living systems framework, the three shifts, the CII, the postures — and produce one real, grounded, systemic action you could take in your own context. Take your time.
1

The signal

What is the city problem you are going to work with?

Remember: Don't describe the symptom — describe the signal behind it. Not "there is too much traffic" but "traffic is a symptom of misaligned land use, mobility culture, and governance fragmentation that has been reinforcing for two decades."

Describe the challenge you're choosing — and the pattern or feedback loop you believe is producing it

Which CII pillar does this challenge most directly touch?

2

The leverage point

Where in the system would a precise, small action shift the whole pattern?

Remember: Leverage hides in structure, not scale. It is rarely the most obvious action. It is often in governance, incentives, feedback mechanisms, or trust infrastructure — the parts of the system that determine how everything else behaves.

Where is the leverage point — the structural location where a small, precise action could shift the pattern?

Why is this a leverage point rather than a symptom-level fix?

3

Your first intelligent move

The smallest action you could take in the next 30 days that would begin to shift this.

Remember: A First Intelligent Move is not the biggest thing you could do. It is the most precisely placed thing you can do now — small enough to be real, precise enough to matter. It creates the conditions for the second move, not just a visible output.

What is your First Intelligent Move — specific, concrete, doable in 30 days?

How will you know it has shifted something — what signal will you look for?

4

Your commitment

The posture you'll bring. The practice you'll sustain.

This is the last question of the course. It asks not what you will do, but who you will be while doing it — and how you will hold the City Intelligence lens when the institutional pressure to revert is strongest.

Which professional posture does your First Intelligent Move require — and why?

What will make this hard — and how will you hold your ground?

One sentence: your commitment to the cities and people you work with
